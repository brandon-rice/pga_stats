pga_stats.combined_data
The combined data table contains each golfer summarized at one line per golfer. 
The data in the table is updated every 1-2 weeks. The player column contains the names of each golfer. 
The columns labeled "SG_last_1" through "SG_last_89" contain the moving average of the shots gained stat. 
DG_rank is the most recent rank from a website called "data golf". Data golf analyzes the performance of golfers and ranks them based on performance. 
Owgr_rank is the world ranking of golfers from the Official world golf ranking organization. 
The refresh_date is the date the pipeline that produces this table has been refreshed. 
The columns labeled "SG_last_1_percentile" through "SG_last_89_percentile" is the percentile rank of each of the "SG_last_x" columns. 
Total tournaments is the count of tournaments a golfer has participated in since ~ 2022. 
tournaments_last_year is the count of golf events that the golfer participated in the last calendar year. 
Cut_percentage_last_year is the percentage of time a golfer made the cut. Each golf tournament has 4 rounds. 
After the second round of a tournament, the top 50% of golfers by score make the cut while the bottom 50% of golfers do not make the cut. 
So the Cut_percentage_last_year column is the percentage of time a golfer "made the cut", in other words they where in the top 50% of golfers at the end of round 2 and were able to play the remaining rounds of the event (rounds 3 and 4). 
Last_3_avg_position is the average finish position of the golfer over the past 3 results. 
The last_5_avg_position and last_10_avg_position follow the same logic as the last_3_avg_position column. 
The top_5_count	top_10_count top_20_count columns are a count of how many times a golfer finished in the top 5, 10 and 20 of golfers for an event in the past year.
The top_5_percentage_last_year	top_10_percentage_last_year	top_20_percentage_last_year columns are the % of times each golfer finished in the top 5,10,20 in the past year. 
The columns: top_5_last_5	top_10_last_5	top_20_last_5 is a count of the amount of times a golfer finished in the top 5,10,20 over the last 5 tournaments. 
The columns: top_5_last_10	top_10_last_10	top_20_last_10 is a count of the amount of times a golfer finished in the top 5,10,20 over the last 10 tournaments. 
The following columns are the percentile ranks for previous columns: cut_percentage_last_year_percentile	top_5_percentage_last_year_percentile	top_10_percentage_last_year_percentile	top_20_percentage_last_year_percentile	top_5_last_5_percentile	top_10_last_5_percentile	top_20_last_5_percentile	top_5_last_10_percentile	top_10_last_10_percentile	top_20_last_10_percentile
The composite_score is a metric that is created by assigning weights to many of the metrics in the table and getting a composite score for each golfer. The higher the value, the higher performing the golfer. 
The composite_percentile_rank column is the percentile rank of the composite_score column / metric. 


pga_stats.sg_data
This table contains golfer stats for stokes gained. After each tournament, the strokes gained are calculated 
for players that make the cut and finish the round. Each row contains the stroke gained stats for a golfer for the tournament. 
The "player" column contains the name of the golfer for the row. 
The avg column is the average value of the total strokes gained metric. The avg is calculated  by dividing the 
total_sg_t by measured rounds. 
The total_sg_t column is the total strokes gained metric. 
The total_sg_t2g is the strokes gained tee to green metric. This is the amount of strokes better this player is compared to other players in the tournament. 
The total_sg_p is the strokes gained putting metric. 
The year column is the year of the event / tournament. 
The tournament column is the name of the tournament the golfer took place in. 
Week_of_season is the week the tournament happened. 
Course is the course the tournament took place on. 
Sigature_event is a flag if the tournament was a Sigature_event. If the field = 'Y' then it was a sigature_event. Signature events have bigger cash prize amounts to the winner and the field is more competitve. 
Major_event column is a flag to indicate if the tournament was major tournament. There are 4 major tournaments a year, the masters, the US open, the PGA Championship and the Open Championship. 


pga_stats.leaderboard_data 
The column "pos" stands for position. This indicates the position the golfer finished for the tournament. 1 is the best position. A player can finish tied for positions, T3 means the golfer tied for third. 
A golfer can also have a record saying "cut" in the pos column, meaning the golfer was cut during the tournament. The pos column can also say w/d with means withdrawl. 
The player column is the name of the golfer. 
r1, r2, r3, r4 indicates the score of the golfer from round 1, round 2, round 3 and round 4. The scores in the r1,r2,r3,r4 is the score to par. A value of -5 in r1 indicates the golfer was 5 shots under par for round 1. 
The "to_par" column is the total and final score of the golfer for the tournament. 
The Official_money column is the amount of money the golfer won for the tournament. 
The year column indicates the year the tournament took place. 
The tournament column is the name of the tournament the golfer took place in. 
Week_of_season is the week the tournament happened. 
Course is the course the tournament took place on. 
Sigature_event is a flag if the tournament was a Sigature_event. If the field = 'Y' then it was a sigature_event. Signature events have bigger cash prize amounts to the winner and the field is more competitve. 
Major_event column is a flag to indicate if the tournament was major tournament. There are 4 major tournaments a year, the masters, the US open, the PGA Championship and the Open Championship.
